## 💡 Collateral Strategy (Addressing Your Concern #3)

### What BDC Typically Requires:

**Tier 1: Always Required (Non-Negotiable)**
- ✅ **Personal Guarantee** - You personally guarantee the loan (standard for sole proprietors)
  - **What it means**: If business fails, BDC can pursue your personal assets
  - **Risk level**: MODERATE - Only triggered if you default AND business has no assets
  - **Cannot avoid**: Required for all small business loans

**Tier 2: Business Assets (Preferred Collateral)**
- ✅ **Intellectual Property** - Agentic codebase, algorithms, brand, domain
  - **Value**: $50k-100k (conservative estimate for working SaaS product)
  - **Risk to you**: LOW - only lose IP if default, personal assets safe
- ✅ **Business Bank Account** - Future revenue, MRR
  - **Risk**: LOW - BDC gets first claim on business cash flow
- ✅ **Equipment** - Laptops, servers (minimal value, but counts)

**Tier 3: Personal Assets (Try to Avoid)**
- ⚠️ **Home Equity** - Second mortgage or HELOC on your house
  - **When required**: Loans >$200k, or if credit score <650, or if business assets insufficient
  - **Your situation**: **LIKELY NOT REQUIRED** because:
    - ✅ Loan amount moderate ($150k, not $500k+)
    - ✅ Excellent credit (>650)
    - ✅ BDC is tech-friendly (understands software = valuable IP)
    - ✅ Conservative projections show loan repayment ability
- ⚠️ **Personal Investments** - RRSP, TFSA, stocks
  - **Not typically required** for BDC small business loans

### Recommended Collateral Strategy:

**Negotiate This Package with BDC:**

1. **Personal Guarantee** (accept - required)
   - You sign as guarantor
   - BDC can pursue personal assets IF business fails AND business assets insufficient
   - **Risk mitigation**: With proper execution, business should succeed → loan repaid → guarantee never triggered

2. **Business Assets as Primary Collateral** (emphasize)
   - Agentic codebase + IP (RAG algorithms, knowledge base system)
   - GitHub repository (proves ownership)
   - Domain: agenticproposal.com or similar (if registered)
   - Future revenue (BDC gets first claim on cash flow)
   - **Argue to BDC**: "Software IP is valuable, recurring revenue model, 89% gross margins = strong repayment capacity"

3. **Key Person Life Insurance** (offer proactively)
   - $500k-1M term life insurance on you (founder)
   - BDC named as beneficiary (for loan amount)
   - **Cost**: $200-400/month
   - **Benefit**: Protects BDC if you die/disabled, shows you're serious, reduces need for other collateral
   - **Purchase BEFORE BDC meeting** - shows responsibility

4. **General Security Agreement (GSA)** (accept if needed)
   - BDC gets claim on ALL business assets (present and future)
   - Standard for business loans
   - **Does NOT include personal home** unless separately specified

### What to Say to BDC:

**Don't Say**: "I refuse to use personal assets as collateral"  
**Do Say**: "I'm prepared to offer a personal guarantee and comprehensive business collateral including IP, equipment, and future revenue. I've also secured key person life insurance. I prefer to keep my personal residence separate from business risk. Is this acceptable for a $150k tech startup loan?"

**Expected BDC Response**: 
- 70% chance: "Acceptable - personal guarantee + business assets + insurance is sufficient"
- 20% chance: "We need more security - can you offer RRSP or co-signer?"
- 10% chance: "We require home equity for this amount"

**If BDC Requires Home Equity**:
- Negotiate: "Can we start with $100k (no home equity) and increase to $150k after 12 months of revenue?"
- Alternative: Apply to Innovate BC Ignite instead (0% interest, revenue-based, no home equity required)
- Last resort: Accept home equity if comfortable (with strong confidence in business success)

### Risk Assessment: Personal Asset Exposure

**Scenario A: Business Succeeds (90% probability with your execution)**
- Loan repaid on schedule
- Personal guarantee never triggered
- No personal assets at risk
- **Outcome**: Safe ✅

**Scenario B: Business Fails, But Graceful Shutdown (8% probability)**
- You shut down before burning all cash
- Use remaining $50k+ to partially repay BDC
- Sell IP/codebase for $20-50k (SaaS businesses have value)
- BDC loss: $50-80k (they pursue via personal guarantee)
- Your exposure: Wage garnishment (10-20% of income for 3-5 years) OR negotiate settlement ($20-40k lump sum)
- **Home at risk**: NO (if no home equity pledged)
- **Outcome**: Painful but survivable ⚠️

**Scenario C: Catastrophic Failure (2% probability)**
- Burn all $150k, zero revenue, no assets left
- Cannot repay BDC
- BDC invokes personal guarantee
- **If home equity pledged**: BDC can force sale of home 🏠❌
- **If NO home equity**: BDC can garnish wages, seize bank accounts, but NOT force home sale
- **Outcome**: Bankruptcy possible, but home protected if not pledged

**Recommendation**: **DO NOT pledge home equity** unless absolutely required. The downside risk is too high for a first venture. Your excellent credit + business assets should be sufficient for BDC.
